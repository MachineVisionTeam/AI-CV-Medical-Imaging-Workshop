Description

This workshop examines the practical integration of artificial intelligence and computer vision into real-world research across various applied fields. Tailored for researchers, engineers, clinicians, and domain experts, the event acts as a collaborative platform to discuss, demonstrate, and develop AI-driven visual solutions that tackle real science and industry healthcare challenges.

Through interdisciplinary talks, hands-on sessions, and interactive discussions, participants will examine how computer vision and AI models are deployed to interpret complex visual data from microscopic images to satellite scans and medical diagnostics. The workshop encourages cross-disciplinary networking and knowledge exchange to catalyze impactful research outcomes.

Workshop Format

Expert Talks & Lectures

Learn from domain experts in AI and medical imaging who will share cutting-edge research, methodologies, and insights into the rapidly evolving field of computational pathology and AI-driven diagnostics.

Hands-on Sessions

Apply deep learning techniques to medical datasets in guided practical sessions. Participants will gain experience with state-of-the-art tools and frameworks for medical image analysis and model development.

Panel Discussion

Explore challenges, solutions, and the future of AI in healthcare through interactive discussions with experts and peers, addressing both technical and ethical considerations in the field.

Join us for a comprehensive learning experience

Bridging the gap between AI technology and medical applications, equipping participants with the latest tools and techniques for medical image analysis.

Key Topics Covered

AI and Computer Vision Foundations

  • Deep learning architectures for vision: CNNs, Vision Transformers, and hybrid models
  • Data-efficient techniques: transfer learning, self-supervised learning, few-shot methods
  • Deployment-ready pipelines for real-time and scalable applications

Domain-Specific Applications

  • Medical Imaging: disease detection, segmentation, diagnostics
  • Environmental Monitoring: satellite image analysis, biodiversity tracking
  • Industrial Vision: defect detection, automation, safety systems
  • Biosciences: microscopy, cell tracking, image-based phenotyping

Data Handling and Annotation

  • Challenges in labeling domain-specific visual data
  • Annotation tools and crowdsourcing
  • Data privacy and governance in sensitive fields (e.g., healthcare)

Responsible and Explainable AI

  • Interpretable models for trust and transparency
  • Addressing bias and ensuring fairness in vision algorithms
  • Regulatory and ethical considerations in applied research

Cross-Disciplinary Collaboration Models

  • Building AI systems with domain experts (e.g., clinicians, biologists)
  • Translating research into deployable solutions
  • Funding, publishing, and scaling interdisciplinary projects

Who Should Attend?

This workshop is ideal for:

  • Undergraduate and graduate students in AI, computer vision, biomedical engineering, and related fields
  • Researchers working in medical imaging and computational pathology
  • Medical professionals and pathologists interested in AI applications
  • Engineers and data scientists developing AI solutions for healthcare
