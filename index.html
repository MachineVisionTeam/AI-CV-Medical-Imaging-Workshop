Description

Medical imaging is a cornerstone of modern healthcare, enabling clinicians and researchers to detect, diagnose, and monitor diseases with high precision. The emergence of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Computer Vision has dramatically transformed medical image analysis, providing automated, data-driven insights that improve efficiency and accuracy.

This one-day interactive workshop will introduce participants to the latest advancements in AI-based medical image analysis, focusing on histopathology, radiology, and multimodal medical imaging. Attendees will gain insights into state-of-the-art deep learning models, image processing techniques, and interpretability methods that are shaping the future of computational pathology and AI-driven diagnostics.

The workshop is designed to balance theoretical concepts with practical, hands-on experience. Expert speakers will cover key topics such as deep learning architectures, feature extraction, segmentation techniques, dataset challenges, and AI ethics in healthcare. Additionally, using tools, participants will be able to train AI models on real-world medical image datasets.

Key Topics Covered

Introduction to AI and Computer Vision in Medical Imaging

  • Fundamentals of AI-driven image analysis
  • Role of deep learning in medical imaging

Medical Image Processing and Preprocessing Techniques

  • Noise reduction, contrast enhancement, and normalization
  • Registration and segmentation in medical images

Deep Learning Architectures for Medical Image Analysis

  • Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) for classification
  • Vision Transformers and their applications

Histopathology Image Analysis with AI

  • Whole Slide Image (WSI) processing
  • Tumor detection and grading

Feature Extraction and Representation Learning

  • Traditional vs. deep learning-based feature extraction
  • Self-supervised and contrastive learning in medical imaging

Explainability and Interpretability in AI for Healthcare

  • Importance of AI explainability in clinical applications
  • Grad-CAM and SHAP for model interpretability

Dataset Challenges and Ethical Considerations

  • Public datasets in medical imaging (TCGA, CAMELYON, etc.)
  • Bias, privacy, and regulatory compliance in AI models

Multi-Modal Data Fusion in Medical Imaging

  • Combining histopathology, radiology, and genomics
  • Multi-modal AI approaches for better diagnosis

Hands-on Session: Training Deep Learning Models for Medical Imaging

  • Practical implementation using TensorFlow/PyTorch
  • Model evaluation metrics and performance analysis

Challenges and Future Trends in AI for Medical Imaging

  • Current limitations and open research questions
  • Future directions in AI-driven healthcare

Who Should Attend?

This workshop is ideal for:

  • Undergraduate and graduate students in AI, computer vision, biomedical engineering, and related fields
  • Researchers working in medical imaging and computational pathology
  • Medical professionals and pathologists interested in AI applications
  • Engineers and data scientists developing AI solutions for healthcare
